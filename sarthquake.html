HOW TO PREPARE FOR: EARTHQUAKES

BEFORE AN EARTHQUAKE:

1. Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan that includes communication strategies, meeting points, and designated roles for each family member. Practice the plan regularly.

2. Secure Heavy Objects: Anchor heavy furniture, appliances, and objects to prevent them from falling and causing injuries during an earthquake.

3. Conduct a Home Safety Check: Identify potential hazards in your home, such as loose wiring or unsecured water heaters, and address them to reduce risks during an earthquake.

4. Prepare an Emergency Kit: Assemble an emergency supply kit that includes essential items like food, water, medications, first aid supplies, flashlights, batteries, and important documents. Store it in a readily accessible location.

5. Educate Yourself: Learn about earthquake safety and response procedures. Understand how to "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" during an earthquake to protect yourself from falling objects and debris.

DURING THE EARTHQUAKE:

1. Drop, Cover, and Hold On: When an earthquake occurs, drop to the ground, take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture, and hold on until the shaking stops. Stay away from windows, heavy furniture that could topple, and objects that may fall.

2. Stay Indoors: If you are indoors, do not attempt to run outside during the shaking. Falling debris and collapsing structures pose significant risks. Stay where you are until it is safe to move.

3. Protect Your Head: If you are unable to take cover under furniture, protect your head and neck with your arms and seek shelter against an interior wall away from windows.

AFTER AN EARTHQUAKE:

1. Check for Injuries: Assess yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid as necessary and seek medical assistance for serious injuries.

2. Be Aware of Aftershocks: Aftershocks often follow the main earthquake. Be prepared for further shaking and take precautions to avoid hazards caused by structural damage or falling debris.

3. Inspect Your Surroundings: Check for hazards such as gas leaks, downed power lines, or damaged structures. Evacuate if necessary and follow instructions from local authorities.

4. Communicate and Seek Assistance: Use text messages or social media to communicate, as these channels are often more reliable during emergencies. Contact emergency services if needed and notify your loved ones about your safety.

5. Document Damage: Take photographs or videos of any damage to your property for insurance claims. Keep records of any necessary repairs or expenses incurred.
