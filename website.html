NATURAL DISASTER AND HAZARD CLASSIFICATIONS

What can we do about these natural disasters

Natural hazards are terrifying forces of nature, capable of causing chaos and destruction. Earthquakes, hurricanes, and tsunamis instill fear in our hearts, reminding us of their devastating power. But amidst the darkness, there is hope. With knowledge, preparation, and self-trust, we can prevent and lessen the impact of these hazards

Natural hazards are classified into 4 types

GEOLOGICAL HAZARDS

Geological Hazards refer to natural events or processes that occur within the Earth's geosphere and pose a threat to human life, property, and infrastructure. These hazards are typically associated with geological phenomena such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and tsunamis. They result from the dynamic nature of the Earth, including tectonic plate movements, volcanic activity, and the gradual processes of erosion and weathering.

METEOROLOGICAL HAZARDS

Meteorological Hazards are natural phenomena related to weather and atmospheric conditions that can pose risks to human life, property, and the environment. These hazards are typically associated with severe weather events such as hurricanes, tornadoes, thunderstorms, blizzards, heatwaves, and floods. They arise from the dynamic interactions of air masses, temperature gradients, moisture content, and atmospheric instability.

CLIMATOLOGICAL HAZARDS

Climatological Hazards refers to hazards and risks associated with long-term weather patterns and climate conditions, such as droughts, heatwaves, cold spells, and extreme precipitation events.

BIOLOGICAL HAZARDS

Biological hazards refers to the potential risks and dangers posed by living organisms or substances derived from them, such as bacteria, viruses, parasites, and fungi.
